Uncorking the World: A New Partnership for Texas Hill Country Wine Lovers

February 28, 2026 By Diane Mack

Texas Wine Country Is Having a Moment

If you haven't been paying attention to what's happening in Texas wine country, now is the time to take notice. The Texas Hill Country has quietly grown into one of the most exciting wine regions in the United States, with over 100 wineries and tasting rooms scattered across the rolling hills between Fredericksburg, Stonewall, and Johnson City.

What started as a handful of pioneering vineyards has blossomed into a full-fledged wine destination, drawing visitors from across the country who come for the award-winning wines and stay for the stunning landscapes, warm hospitality, and farm-to-table dining that define this part of Texas.

And now, I'm thrilled to bring the world of wine even closer to the world of travel.

A New Partnership: Wine Meets Wanderlust

I'm excited to announce that Travel Creations By Di has partnered with a curated selection of Texas Hill Country vineyards to offer something truly special: exclusive wine-and-travel experiences that combine the best of both worlds.

"Great wine and great travel have something in common — they're both about savoring the moment, discovering something new, and creating memories that stay with you long after the last sip."

This partnership means I can now help you plan vineyard visits, private tastings, and wine trail weekends as part of a broader travel itinerary — or as a standalone getaway right here in our beautiful Hill Country backyard.

What This Looks Like for You

Here are just a few of the experience packages we're putting together:

Wine Trail Weekends

A curated two- or three-day itinerary along the Fredericksburg Wine Road 290 corridor. I'll map out the perfect route through four to six tasting rooms, arrange reservations at the ones that require them, and book you into a charming local inn or vacation rental. You just show up and enjoy.

Vineyard Tours + Vacation Getaways

Pair a Hill Country wine experience with a bigger trip. Spending a few days in San Antonio or Austin? Let me add a day trip to wine country as part of your itinerary — complete with transportation recommendations, tasting appointments, and a farm-to-table dinner reservation to cap it off.

Group Wine Getaways

Planning a girls' weekend, a couples' retreat, or a birthday celebration? Wine country is the perfect setting. I'll coordinate group-friendly wineries, book accommodations that keep everyone together, and handle the logistics so the host can actually relax and enjoy the trip.

Wine + Wider Travel

Love wine and love to travel? Let me build wine experiences into your next vacation anywhere in the world. From Napa and Sonoma to Tuscany, Bordeaux, or the Douro Valley in Portugal, I can pair vineyard visits with your broader travel plans for a trip that feeds every sense.

The Fredericksburg-Stonewall-Johnson City Wine Trail

For those new to Texas Hill Country wine, here's a quick lay of the land. The heart of the wine region runs along US-290 between Johnson City and Fredericksburg, with Stonewall sitting right in the middle. This stretch is sometimes called "Wine Road 290," and for good reason — you'll find dozens of tasting rooms within a few miles of each other.

A few highlights along the trail:

  • Fredericksburg — The anchor of Hill Country wine country. Beyond the wineries, the town itself is a destination: German heritage architecture, boutique shopping on Main Street, and some of the best restaurants in the region.
  • Stonewall — Home to several acclaimed vineyards and the LBJ Ranch. The quieter pace here makes it ideal for a leisurely afternoon of tasting.
  • Johnson City — A growing wine hub with a more laid-back feel. Several newer wineries have opened here with beautiful tasting rooms and Hill Country views.

Beyond the Glass: Local Recommendations

A great wine trip is about more than just the wine. Here's what I love to build into a Hill Country wine itinerary:

  • Farm-to-table dining — The Hill Country food scene has exploded in recent years. From wood-fired pizza at a vineyard to refined Texas cuisine in downtown Fredericksburg, the dining options are exceptional.
  • Scenic drives — Willow City Loop, the Enchanted Rock road, and the back roads between Llano and Mason are gorgeous any time of year — and absolutely spectacular during wildflower season.
  • Local markets and shops — Pick up artisan cheeses, Texas olive oil, local honey, and handmade goods to bring a piece of the Hill Country home with you.
  • Lavender farms — Several lavender farms dot the area around Blanco and Fredericksburg, offering tours, products, and beautiful photo opportunities, especially in June.

This Is Just the Beginning

I've always believed that the best travel experiences come from connecting with a place on a deeper level — through its food, its people, and its landscape. Wine is one of the most beautiful ways to do that. A glass of Tempranillo from a Stonewall vineyard tells a story about this soil, this climate, and the people who tend these vines. That's the kind of connection I want to help you make.

Whether you're a seasoned wine enthusiast or someone who simply enjoys a good glass with a great view, I'd love to help you plan a Hill Country wine experience — or weave wine country into your next big trip anywhere in the world.
