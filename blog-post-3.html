Hitting a High Note: A Deluxe-to-Value Stay at Disney's All-Star Music Resort

January 15, 2026 By Diane Mack

The Best-Kept Secret at Walt Disney World

Here's something most first-time Disney families don't realize: you don't have to stay at a deluxe resort to enjoy the deluxe experience. One of my favorite strategies for clients — and one I use for my own family trips — is what I call the "Deluxe-to-Value" approach. You book a value resort for your room, then spend your dining and leisure time enjoying the stunning pools, restaurants, and lobbies at Disney's deluxe properties. It's the best of both worlds, and All-Star Music Resort is the perfect home base to make it work.

All-Star Music is one of Disney's three All-Star value resorts, and it holds a special place in my heart. The musical theming is playful without being overwhelming — giant cowboy boots, enormous drum kits, and towering jukeboxes line the courtyards in a way that makes kids light up and adults smile. The rooms are clean, comfortable, and recently refurbished, and the resort's location makes it easy to hop on Disney transportation to any park.

Why the Deluxe-to-Value Strategy Works

Disney's deluxe resorts — think the Grand Floridian, Animal Kingdom Lodge, and the Polynesian Village — are stunning properties with world-class dining, breathtaking lobbies, and resort experiences that feel like destinations in their own right. But a night at a deluxe resort can easily run $500 to $800, while a value resort room at All-Star Music starts around $125 to $175. That price difference adds up fast over a week-long trip.

The beauty of the Deluxe-to-Value strategy is that Disney's resorts are open to all guests, not just those staying there. You can walk into the Grand Floridian's lobby, have dinner at 'Ohana at the Polynesian, watch the fireworks from the beach at the Contemporary, or explore the African-inspired savanna viewing areas at Animal Kingdom Lodge — all without spending a single night at those properties. You get to experience the magic, then head back to your affordable, perfectly comfortable room at All-Star Music.

"A Disney vacation should feel magical from start to finish — and that doesn't require the most expensive room on property. It requires a smart plan."

Making the Most of All-Star Music

All-Star Music has more charm than people give it credit for. Here are a few tips to maximize your stay:

  • Request a preferred room. For a small upcharge, preferred rooms put you closer to the main building, pool, and bus stop. After a long park day, that shorter walk makes a real difference.
  • Take advantage of the food court. The Intermission Food Court serves solid breakfast and dinner options, and the prices are significantly lower than table-service dining. Use the savings to splurge on one or two signature meals at deluxe resorts instead.
  • Enjoy the pools. The Calypso Pool — shaped like a giant guitar — is a highlight for kids. Arrive in the early afternoon when most families are still at the parks and you'll often have it nearly to yourself.
  • Use Disney transportation strategically. All-Star Music is serviced by Disney buses. Plan your deluxe resort dining experiences on park-hopper days or rest days when you're already moving between properties.

Sample Deluxe-to-Value Itinerary

Here's how a typical day might look with this approach: Wake up at All-Star Music and grab a quick breakfast at the food court. Head to Magic Kingdom for a morning of rides and character meet-and-greets. In the late afternoon, hop the monorail to the Polynesian Village Resort for a leisurely dinner at 'Ohana, watching the sunset over Seven Seas Lagoon. After dinner, stroll through the Grand Floridian's gorgeous lobby, maybe catch the live jazz band, then take the bus back to All-Star Music for a restful night's sleep. You've just had a deluxe-caliber day at a fraction of the cost.

This is exactly the kind of planning I love to do for my clients. Every family has different priorities — some want character dining, some want the best pool experience, some want adults-only evenings at Victoria & Albert's. I build itineraries around what matters most to you, making sure the budget stretches as far as the magic does.

Let's Plan Your Disney Strategy

The Deluxe-to-Value approach isn't just about saving money — though it absolutely does that. It's about experiencing more of Walt Disney World than most guests ever see. The resorts are a huge part of the Disney experience, and most families never set foot in any property other than their own. With a little planning, you can enjoy the best of everything Disney has to offer while keeping your vacation budget exactly where you want it.
